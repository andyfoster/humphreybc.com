Benjamin Humphrey

kiwi interface designer & web developer

Specialising in visual and interaction design for applications, I'm a very passionate designer who gets behind what I work on. I have extensive experience designing for web and mobile at numerous startups, including my own company.

I had a big involvement in open source straight after high school and contributed to the Ubuntu community, as well as attending and speaking about design at various Linux conferences and meetups around Australasia.

AVOS rebranding

As a Designer working at AVOS, I worked on the company rebranding, which included extensive print and web design. I also helped build our new website using Node.js and responsive CSS at avos.com.

OMG! Ubuntu!

As the co-founder and an Editor on the site, I designed the visual style for OMG! Ubuntu!, a web magazine that caters to over 250,000 regular readers and gets over one million unique views a month.

Jigsoar

In October 2011 I was hired full time by Dunedin based web design agency Loop Solutions to work on their content management system, Jigsoar.

I was responsible for the interface design, identity design, application behaviour, testing, and documentation of Jigsoar.

Jigsoar icon set

As part of the interface overhaul of Jigsoar, I decided to design an entirely new vector icon set from scratch and make it available as a Creative Commons licensed public download.

It ended up being featured on many popular design blogs and downloaded thousands of times.

Trackr

Late in 2011 and frustrated with looking for a suitable bug tracker for Jigsoar, I decided to begin work on our own one at Loop Solutions.

I conceived Trackr, its interface, design, logo, and name, as well as worked on some front end development.

Pocketsmith

In May 2011 I was contracted by PocketSmith to redesign their product website and provide interface consultation for their web application.

The focus for this project was on user conversions, and in my design I worked hard to ensure a clear and easy sign up process was apparent for visitors. I did not work on the illustrations.

Ubuntu Manual Project

In 2009 I founded the Ubuntu Manual Project, an open-source documentation initiative for the Ubuntu operating system.

I was Project Leader for twelve months, during which time I was in charge of web design and contributed to the print design of the manual itself.

High Performance Sport New Zealand
